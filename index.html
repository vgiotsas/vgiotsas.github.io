About

Vasileios (Vasilis) Giotsas is a research engineer working on network measurements and telemetry, risk assessment and mitigation of Internet infrastructure vulnerabilities, and analysis of Internet routing policies and performance. He's a lecturer (assistant professor) at the Computer and Communications department of Lancaster University, and has worked as a research scientist at the Internet Measurement and Analysis group of TU Berlin, and as a postdoc researcher at CAIDA (Center of Advanced Internet Data Analysis) at UC San Diego. He has worked on multiple research projects funded by the UK National Cyber Security Center (NCSC), the US Department of Homeland Security (DHS), and the Space and Naval Warfare Systems Command (SPAWAR). His work has been recognized with the CoNEXT 2015 best paper award, and the IETF Applied Networking Research Prize 2016. His datasets have been shortlisted in 2013 and 2014 for the ACM SIGCOMM Community Contribution Award. He received two RIPE NCC Community Projects funds and two RIPE NCC fellowships.
